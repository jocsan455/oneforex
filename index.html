What is a PAMM Platform?

PAMM is the acronym for Percentage Allocation Management Module. It is a state-of-the-art technological investment platform provided by some brokers to asset managers, in order to allow them to simultaneously manage the funds of their investors. However, the broker maintains the administrative separation of each investor's funds, including, guaranteeing the right of each one to withdraw their funds at any time, in whole or in part and without the need for third-party authorization. The profits or losses resulting from the operations of the asset manager will be distributed proportionally among the investors, based on the capital individually invested in the PAMM. For his professional work, the asset manager will charge a performance fee (commission) which will be applied to the net profits obtained at the end of each month. Said performance fee will be automatically deducted and credited to the fund manager's account. EXAMPLE:

  • Investors:
  • Invester A:
  • Invester B
  • Invester C
  • Invester D
  • Capital of investors:
  • Investment:
  • $10,000
  • $20,000
  • $40,000
  • $80,000
  • $150,000
  • Participation:
  • 6.67%
  • 13.34%
  • 26.66%
  • 53.33%
  • 100%

If at the end of the month $15,000 of net profit has been generated and the performance fee of the asset manager was agreed upon 30%, the asset manager's fees will be: $15,000 X 30% = $4,500. The resulting net profits will be: $10,500, which will be distributed automatically among investors, as follows:

  • Investors:
  • Invester A:
  • Invester B
  • Invester C
  • Invester D
  • Benefit x Participation:
  • $10,500 x 6.67%
  • $10,500 x 13.34%
  • $10,500 x 26.66%
  • $10,500 x 53.33%
  • Net Profit:
  • $700.35
  • $1,400.70
  • $2,799.30
  • $5,599.65

For the convenience of investors, the high water-mark policy applies. This means that if the asset manager has not generated profits at the end of the month, he will not receive his performance fee.

What is the High Water-Mark policy and how does it work?

It is a formula to calculate the fees of asset managers and protect investors from paying unfair fees. The high water-mark policy guarantees the investor that he will only pay fees to the manager if his investment has experienced a net growth. For a better understanding, below is an example of how the high water-mark policy works:

Suppose that you invested in one of our PAMM platforms an amount of $10,000 and that our performance fee is 30%. If at the end of the first month, the balance of your account closes at $11,000, the net gain will be $1,000 and the performance fee of “One Forex Signal” will be $300 ($1,000 x 30%). The high water-mark will be set at $10,700 (Result: $ 11,000 - $ 300). Then, if by the second month your account ends with a loss of -$400, the current balance will become $10,300 (Result: $10,700 - $400) and “One Forex Signal” will not receive a performance fee in that second month. The high water-mark continues to be established at $10,700. This means that “One Forex Signal” can only receive a commission again, if at the end of the third month (or any of the following months), the balance becomes greater than $10,700. Now, suppose that at the end of the third month the balance ends at $ 12,500. To determine the result, the following calculation will be made: $12,500 - $10,700 - $400 = $1,400. Therefore, the profit of the third month will be $1,400 and the performance fee of “One Forex Signal” will be $420 (Result: $ 1,400 x 30%).
